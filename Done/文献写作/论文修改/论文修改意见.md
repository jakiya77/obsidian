Reviewer: 1  
  
Comments to the Author  

This paper applies MA and FDA to secure communication systems, which is a timely and promising topic. The contribution of this paper is solid. The reviewer has some suggestions for improving the presentation as follows:  

1. It is unclear why the BSUM framework is selected for optimizing the antenna position vector (APV) and antenna frequency vector (AFV) in this work. Since APV and AFV have different physical interpretations, it would be helpful to clarify whether applying BSUM to these two variables leads to fundamentally different optimization behaviors.  
2. The manuscript adopts a BSUM-based optimization strategy for solving the nonconvex problem. Please clarify whether there is any specific structural property or constraint of the formulated problem that motivates the use of BSUM, and briefly explain the principle behind constructing the convex surrogate function (e.g., around Eq. (18)/(23)).  
3. What are the main advantages of the proposed MFDA compared with conventional phased array (PA) and frequency diverse array (FDA) schemes? In particular, how is the concept of two-dimensional covertness realized in the proposed framework? It would also be useful to comment on whether MFDA can still outperform FDA when the frequency constraint is relaxed.  
4. In the proposed MFDA framework, antenna frequency and position are jointly optimized. However, their relative contributions to covert performance are not clearly discussed. Some qualitative insight into the respective roles of frequency optimization and position optimization would improve understanding.  
5. Some sentences in the manuscript use non-standard grammatical constructions. For example, the sentence “We cannot get the expression of the optimal ABV in closed form from problem (13)” is awkward in academic writing and should be revised for clarity.  
6. The two-stage AO algorithm is clearly described, but the interaction between the BSUM procedure and the AO framework is somewhat unclear. Please clarify the respective roles of BSUM and AO in the overall algorithm design.  
7. The conclusion mainly summarizes the results of this paper. A brief comment on possible future research directions related to the proposed MFDA framework would help place this work in a broader context.

Comments to the Author  
The paper is well written and clear, despite the complexity of the scenario. The topic is novel and timely and the results verify the benefits of the methodology. The techniques used are very interesting and hence I suggest acceptance.  
  
There is an impression that the paper is somewhat unmotivated - it feels like the combination of several technologies in a secrecy problem have been put together and solved via AO methods without any particular reason for their use.  
  
  
  
Reviewer: 3  
  
Comments to the Author  
Major Revision:  
1. The channel model assumes perfect and instantaneous knowledge of all Eve positions, which is impractical; please analyze the algorithm's **robustness to imperfect CSI and propose a robust optimization framework or worst-case design.**  

2. The alternating optimization between beamforming, APV, and AFV lacks a **proof of convergence** to at least a local optimum; please provide a theoretical convergence analysis or include numerical evidence (e.g., objective function value trajectory) over multiple random initializations.  

3. The simulation setup assumes three Eves at fixed, known positions; to demonstrate generalizability, please test the algorithm under **more challenging scenarios**, such as **random Eve distributions**, a larger number of Eves, or mobile Eves.  

4. Please comment the following related work in the introduction:  
An Efficient Algorithm for Sum-Rate Maximization in Fluid Antenna-Assisted ISAC System  
  
Reviewer: 4  
  
Comments to the Author  
This paper proposes a 2D-MFDA transmit architecture and investigates its secure transmission performance in the presence of multiple Eves. A two stage AO algorithm is developed to maximize the secrecy capacity by jointly optimizing transmit beamforming, APV, and AFV. This work is relevant and interesting. Some suggestions are given as follows:  

4. The system model figure should be improved to clearly demonstrate the working mechanism of the proposed 2D-MFDA transmit architecture, rather than only showing the operation principle of the MA.  
5. The rank-one constraint of the transmit beamforming is not explicitly considered. Please explain whether a relaxation method is applied and how the rank-one solution is guaranteed.  
6. The proposed two stage AO algorithm first optimizes APV and AFV, and then optimizes the transmit beamforming. The authors should clarify the reason of the two-stage design.  
7. The calculation of the upper bound shown in Fig. 3 is not clearly explained. Please provide the corresponding equations or computational details.  

8. PLS and flexible-antenna techniques are hot research topics in recent years. The authors are suggested to enrich the state-of-the-art part in Section I, for example the follows:  
[R1] "Near-Field Integrated Sensing and Communications for Secure UAV Networks," in IEEE Journal on Selected Areas in Communications.  
  
  
Reviewer: 5  
  
Comments to the Author  
In this letter, the authors investigate a two-dimensional movable frequency-diverse array (2D-MFDA) transmit architecture for physical-layer security in MISOME systems. By jointly designing the beamforming vector, element-wise frequency offsets, and antenna positions to maximize the secrecy rate, the authors address a non-convex optimization problem using a two-block alternating-optimization (AO) algorithm. Simulation results indicate that enlarging the movable region provides significant performance gains for the proposed 2D-MFDA transmit architecture.  
  
After a careful reading, the Reviewer would like to raise the following major and minor comments.  
  
9. The Reviewer suggests that the authors discuss recently published magazine- or tutorial-style papers on physical-layer security, particularly those related to 6G systems, to further strengthen and update the literature review.  
  
10. **The Reviewer strongly suggests that the authors justify their adoption of line-of-sight (LoS) channels in the system model and clearly specify the type of small-scale fading considered. Moreover, if only LoS channels are assumed, the authors should explain why different path-loss exponents are used for the main channel and the eavesdroppers’ channels**.  
  
11. The Reviewer suggests that the authors clarify that the eavesdroppers considered in this work are assumed to be non-collaborative and provide a justification for this assumption.  
  
12. The Reviewer suggests that the authors check the **correctness of Eq. (8).** In particular, the operator [" " ⓜ⋅" " ]^+ ┤should be included in the expression for the secrecy capacity.  
  
13. **The Reviewer strongly suggests that the authors justify the use of very high SNRs in the numerical results. Specifically, the transmit power is set to 10 dBm while the noise power is −100 dBm, corresponding to an average SNR of 110 dB, which is unrealistically high for most practical wireless communication systems.**  
  
14. **The Reviewer strongly suggests that all acronyms be clearly and correctly defined before their first use. For example, in the Abstract, “MISOME” should be defined in parentheses, while in Section I, “PLS” and “RIS” should be defined before being used.**  
  
15. The Reviewer suggests that the authors use the word “respectively” in a correct and professional manner, for example in the descriptions of Eqs. (2) and (3), Eqs. (4) and (5), and Eqs. (6) and (7). As an illustration, the text around Eqs. (2) and (3) may be revised as follows:  
The channel from Alice to Bob and the channel from Alice to the kth Eve are defined as  
… (2)  
and  
… (3),  
respectively, where …  
  
  
In addition, the sentence “We cannot get the expression of the optimal …” at the beginning of Section III-B could be revised to “We cannot obtain the expression for the optimal …”.  
  
16. The Reviewer suggests that the authors consistently use the present perfect or past tense when describing contributions of existing studies, for example revising “System-level studies further demonstrate …” to “System-level studies have further demonstrated …” in Section I. In addition, the Reviewer suggests avoiding the use of the future tense in Section IV, as the present tense is sufficient. The authors are also encouraged to use hyphens appropriately (e.g., “non-convex” instead of “nonconvex” in the Abstract), revise unprofessional or overly long sentences (e.g., splitting the sentence “Movable antennas allow … secrecy rate improves [18]” into two shorter sentences), and rewrite the notations paragraph in a consistent and professional manner.  
  
17. The Reviewer strongly suggests that the authors strictly adhere to the IEEE reference formatting guidelines. For instance, proper nouns such as “5G,” “RIS-ISAC,” “6-D,” and “CVX” should be capitalized. Journal abbreviations should be used consistently, and the publication month of journal articles should be included. Moreover, some cited works (e.g., [9], [16], and [17]) appear to have been published; the authors are encouraged to carefully verify and update their publication information accordingly.
