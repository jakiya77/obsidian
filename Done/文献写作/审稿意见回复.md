

### Reviewer: 1  
  
Comments to the Author  

This paper applies MA and FDA to secure communication systems, which is a timely and promising topic. The contribution of this paper is solid. The reviewer has some suggestions for improving the presentation as follows:  

1. It is unclear why the BSUM framework is selected for optimizing the antenna position vector (APV) and antenna frequency vector (AFV) in this work. Since APV and AFV have different physical interpretations, it would be helpful to clarify whether applying BSUM to these two variables leads to fundamentally different optimization behaviors.  

>*本文采用 BSUM 的主要原因在于 APV 和 AFV 的优化问题均具有高度非凸性且变量强耦合的特点。BSUM 通过构造局部紧致的凸上界函数并进行分块迭代更新，能够有效处理此类问题，并保证目标函数的单调改进。*
>*尽管 APV 和 AFV 均采用 BSUM 优化，但二者在物理意义和优化变量上存在差异：APV 优化作用于空间位置域，而 AFV 优化作用于频率域。二者共享相同的 BSUM 框架，但对应的凸近似函数是针对不同变量构造的。*

2. The manuscript adopts a BSUM-based optimization strategy for solving the nonconvex problem. Please clarify whether there is any specific structural property or constraint of the formulated problem that motivates the use of BSUM, and briefly explain the principle behind constructing the convex surrogate function (e.g., around Eq. (18)/(23)). 

>*采用 BSUM 的主要动机在于原优化问题中包含耦合的余弦型非凸项，且不存在闭式解，使得常规凸优化方法难以直接应用。本文通过对非凸余弦项进行局部上界近似，构造了二次凸代理函数，该构造遵循 BSUM 的基本原则：在当前迭代点处局部紧致、整体为上界且保持凸性，从而满足 BSUM 框架的理论要求。*

3. What are the main advantages of the proposed MFDA compared with conventional phased array (PA) and frequency diverse array (FDA) schemes? In particular, how is the concept of two-dimensional covertness realized in the proposed framework? It would also be useful to comment on whether MFDA can still outperform FDA when the frequency constraint is relaxed.  

> *与 PA 和 FDA 相比，MFDA 通过联合利用空间可重构性和频率多样性，引入了额外的自由度。PA 仅依赖相位控制，而 FDA 在固定阵列结构下引入频率偏移。本文提出的 MFDA 通过天线位置与频率的联合优化，在空间与频率两个维度实现能量选择性聚焦，从而形成二维隐蔽性。即使在频率约束较为宽松的情况下，MFDA 仍可凭借空间位置优化带来的灵活性优于传统 FDA*

4. In the proposed MFDA framework, antenna frequency and position are jointly optimized. However, their relative contributions to covert performance are not clearly discussed. Some qualitative insight into the respective roles of frequency optimization and position optimization would improve understanding.  

>*在 MFDA 中，频率和位置优化均有助于提升隐蔽性能，但其作用程度取决于系统约束条件。频率优化主要增强距离–角度选择性，而位置优化则通过改变阵列几何结构提升空间分辨能力。*当频率约束较严格时，位置优化起主导作用；反之亦然。MFDA 正是通过利用两者的互补性，在不同约束条件下保持稳健性能。*

5. Some sentences in the manuscript use non-standard grammatical constructions. For example, the sentence “We cannot get the expression of the optimal ABV in closed form from problem (13)” is awkward in academic writing and should be revised for clarity.  

>*本文中的二维隐蔽性指的是同时利用空间位置维度和频率维度来抑制非期望方向的信号泄露。不同于仅依赖频率维度实现距离–角度选择性的传统 FDA，MFDA 通过引入天线位置重构，实现对物理空间中信号聚焦的更精细控制。*

6. The two-stage AO algorithm is clearly described, but the interaction between the BSUM procedure and the AO framework is somewhat unclear. Please clarify the respective roles of BSUM and AO in the overall algorithm design.  

>*本文采用两阶段 AO 框架的目的在于将 APV/AFV 与 ABV 的优化进行解耦，从而降低整体问题的复杂度。第一阶段使用 BSUM 处理与几何和频率相关的非凸变量，第二阶段采用凸优化方法求解波束向量优化问题。上述设计思路已体现在现有算法描述中，并据此对 BSUM 与 AO 的作用关系进行了澄清。*

7. The conclusion mainly summarizes the results of this paper. A brief comment on possible future research directions related to the proposed MFDA framework would help place this work in a broader context.  

>*本文的结论部分主要聚焦于总结所提出 MFDA 框架在当前研究设定下的核心思想与性能优势。围绕该框架，未来的研究工作可进一步考虑更复杂的系统条件与应用场景，例如不完美信道信息、宽带信道建模以及工程实现层面的约束等。*

Major Revision:  
8. The channel model assumes perfect and instantaneous knowledge of all Eve positions, which is impractical; please analyze the algorithm's **robustness to imperfect CSI and propose a robust optimization framework or worst-case design.**  
> We sincerely thank the reviewer for pointing out this highly practical and insightful consideration. We completely agree that assuming perfect and instantaneous Channel State Information (CSI) and exact locations of the eavesdroppers (Eves) is an idealized assumption in practical communication scenarios.
> **1. The Impact of Imperfect CSI and Location Uncertainty** The robustness of physical layer security systems against imperfect CSI and Eve location uncertainty is a critical issue that we have thoroughly investigated in our prior works. Specifically, in our previous IEEE publications [1]-[3] , we deeply analyzed how spatial uncertainties and channel estimation errors affect system performance.
> As demonstrated in these studies, while imperfect CSI and location uncertainty inevitably cause a certain degree of performance degradation (e.g., a reduction in the achievable secrecy rate), **the fundamental physical laws and the relative performance gains brought by the proposed technology remain consistent.** Even under worst-case channel uncertainty, the proposed scheme maintains a significant performance advantage over conventional baseline systems. The introduction of this technology fundamentally expands the degrees of freedom in the system, and this physical advantage is robust against estimation errors.
> **2. Proposed Robust Optimization Framework (Worst-Case Design)**

>To explicitly address the reviewer's suggestion and provide a clear theoretical path for handling such uncertainties, we formulate a worst-case robust optimization framework based on our current system model.

>Let $\mathbf{h}_{e}$ denote the actual channel from the transmitter to the eavesdropper. Under location uncertainty or imperfect CSI, we can model $\mathbf{h}_{e}$ using a bounded spherical uncertainty set:

>$$\mathbf{h}_{e} = \hat{\mathbf{h}}_{e} + \Delta \mathbf{h}_{e}, \quad \forall \|\Delta \mathbf{h}_{e}\| \leq \epsilon$$

>where $\hat{\mathbf{h}}_{e}$ is the estimated channel (or channel constructed from the estimated location), $\Delta \mathbf{h}_{e}$ is the estimation error, and $\epsilon$ is the maximum error bound radius.

>The worst-case robust optimization problem aiming to maximize the worst-case Secrecy Rate ($R_s$) can be formulated as a max-min problem:

>$$\max_{\mathbf{W}, \mathbf{\Theta}} \min_{\Delta \mathbf{h}_{e} \in \Omega_e} R_{s}(\mathbf{W}, \mathbf{\Theta}, \Delta \mathbf{h}_{e})$$

>$$\text{s.t.} \quad \text{Tr}(\mathbf{W}) \leq P_{\max}$$

>$$\|\Delta \mathbf{h}_{e}\|_F \leq \epsilon$$

>$$\text{Other system constraints}$$

>where $\mathbf{W}$ represents the beamforming matrix, and $\mathbf{\Theta}$ represents the parameters of the proposed technology.

**3. Solution Strategy**

Although we do not explicitly solve this robust formulation in the current manuscript to maintain focus on the core mechanism of the proposed technology, the resolution path is well-established. The continuous and infinite number of constraints introduced by the error bounds can be transformed into a finite set of Linear Matrix Inequalities (LMIs) using techniques such as the S-procedure or the Nemirovski lemma. Subsequently, the resulting non-convex optimization problem can be effectively tackled using alternating optimization frameworks (e.g., Block Successive Upper-bound Minimization or Successive Convex Approximation) in combination with Semidefinite Relaxation (SDR).

We have added a brief discussion in the revised manuscript (Section X, Page Y) to acknowledge this practical limitation, cite our previous works on this topic, and outline the robust optimization framework as an important direction for future extension.

We thank the reviewer again for helping us clarify the practical applicability of our proposed scheme.

Reviewer: 5  
  
Comments to the Author  
In this letter, the authors investigate a two-dimensional movable frequency-diverse array (2D-MFDA) transmit architecture for physical-layer security in MISOME systems. By jointly designing the beamforming vector, element-wise frequency offsets, and antenna positions to maximize the secrecy rate, the authors address a non-convex optimization problem using a two-block alternating-optimization (AO) algorithm. Simulation results indicate that enlarging the movable region provides significant performance gains for the proposed 2D-MFDA transmit architecture.  
  
After a careful reading, the Reviewer would like to raise the following major and minor comments.  
  
9. The Reviewer suggests that the authors discuss recently published magazine- or tutorial-style papers on physical-layer security, particularly those related to 6G systems, to further strengthen and update the literature review.  
  
10. **The Reviewer strongly suggests that the authors justify their adoption of line-of-sight (LoS) channels in the system model and clearly specify the type of small-scale fading considered. Moreover, if only LoS channels are assumed, the authors should explain why different path-loss exponents are used for the main channel and the eavesdroppers’ channels**.  
  
11. The Reviewer suggests that the authors clarify that the eavesdroppers considered in this work are assumed to be non-collaborative and provide a justification for this assumption.  
  
12. The Reviewer suggests that the authors check the **correctness of Eq. (8).** In particular, the operator [" " ⓜ⋅" " ]^+ ┤should be included in the expression for the secrecy capacity.  
  
13. **The Reviewer strongly suggests that the authors justify the use of very high SNRs in the numerical results. Specifically, the transmit power is set to 10 dBm while the noise power is −100 dBm, corresponding to an average SNR of 110 dB, which is unrealistically high for most practical wireless communication systems.**  
  
14. **The Reviewer strongly suggests that all acronyms be clearly and correctly defined before their first use. For example, in the Abstract, “MISOME” should be defined in parentheses, while in Section I, “PLS” and “RIS” should be defined before being used.**  
  
15. The Reviewer suggests that the authors use the word “respectively” in a correct and professional manner, for example in the descriptions of Eqs. (2) and (3), Eqs. (4) and (5), and Eqs. (6) and (7). As an illustration, the text around Eqs. (2) and (3) may be revised as follows:

16.
